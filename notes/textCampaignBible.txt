Session 4
SESSION 4: THE SCALES OF BASILIA
PART 1: THE MORNING AFTER & TIZZLE’S WARNING
The Scene: The party wakes up at the Rusty Hook Inn. They have an impending court date today for Toern’s crimes.
* Optional Visit (Tizzle's Shop): If the players visit Tizzle before court, he reveals he has been stealing the strange potions but wants nothing to do with them anymore.
* Tizzle's Warning: He explains that one of his munchkins drank a stolen potion and mutated into something monstrous. Tizzle had to kill the creature himself (casually implying Tizzle possesses immense physical strength). This plants the seed for the dangerous anomalies.
PART 2: THE COURT OF COUNSELOR QUESSEL
The Scene: The party arrives at the Basilia Manor for Toern’s hearing. The courtroom is lavish, but the air is tense.
* NPC Roster:
   * Counselor Quessel: Smug, corrupt, and entirely in the pocket of the Jewl Pirates. He wants to make an example of Toern.
   * Captain Pavel: Stands in the gallery. He looks regretful and gives the party a knowing, helpless glance. He cannot interfere here.
* The Trial (Roleplay): Allow Kehrfuffle to step up and represent Toern. Run this as a skill challenge (Persuasion/Deception/Performance).
* The Branching Verdict:
   * If the Party Wins (Innocent): Quessel sighs in frustration, legally forced to clear Toern's name. The courtroom doors immediately burst open.
   * If the Party Loses (Guilty): Quessel smugly sentences the party to the Manor cellars. Before the guards can take them, the courtroom doors burst open.
PART 3: THE WRATH OF JEWL (NON-LETHAL BRAWL)
The Scene: Dreaded Pirate Jewl and her massive goons enter the courtroom. Leeta is with her and points the party out.
* Jewl's Motivation: * (If Innocent): Jewl is furious that Quessel failed to imprison them and decides to put the party in their place herself.
   * (If Guilty): Jewl wants to personally humiliate them and take a souvenir before they rot in the cellars.
* The Combat (Unwinnable): Jewl and her goons are ridiculously strong. Jewl casually dismisses the party's strength. She is not trying to kill them; she is establishing dominance.
* The Theft Mechanic (Crucial): The moment a PC is reduced to 0 Hit Points, Jewl uses her turn to walk over, snatch a valuable item/weapon from their unconscious body, and laugh.
* The Blackout: Once the point is made (or the party is wiped out), Jewl's crew swarms them. The party blacks out.
PART 4: THE MANOR CELLARS (DUNGEON CRAWL)
The Scene: The players wake up at 1 HP (or after a Short Rest) locked in the dark, confusing tunnels beneath the Manor. It serves as both a prison and the Jewl Pirates' secret smuggling vault.
* The Objective: Break out of the cell, navigate the dungeon, and find Lord Pooty.
* Stealth & Puzzles: The players must use skill checks and thieves tools to bypass security and sneak past Crown Guards/Pirates. (Insert your specific puzzles/patrols here).
PART 5: THE EXECUTIONER KORT (BOSS FIGHT)
The Scene: The party reaches the deepest hold. Lord Pooty is locked in a cell. Standing outside is the massive Orc, Kort, accompanied by his goons.
* The Hostage Trigger (Crucial): At the top of initiative, Kort makes his terms clear. If the party kills a goon, Kort will immediately turn and attack Lord Pooty. * The Combat: The party must carefully focus-fire Kort (dealing with his hook-hand and weapon-swapping mechanics) while enduring damage from the goons to keep Pooty safe. Kort fights dirty.
* The Fate of Lord Pooty:
   * If Pooty is rescued: He reveals the critical clue—the cane stolen from Mayor Tudul contains the location of a dormant Nevari. Jewl wants it to trade to Arteme.
   * If Pooty is killed by Kort: Pooty delivers this exact same information to the party with his dying breath.
PART 6: THE ESCAPE & PAVEL’S CLOAK
The Scene: The party flees the Manor and slips out of the city limits, successfully completing the prison break.
* The Rendezvous: They run into Captain Pavel outside of town.
* The Warning: Pavel emphasizes that they are now wanted fugitives. They have drawn the ire of both the Crown and the Jewl Pirates. If they split up, they will die.
* The Cloak: Pavel gifts them an item/scroll of cloaking magic to help them evade capture. He urges them to run far away from Basilia.
* The Player Choice: The party has true agency. They can use the cloak to flee into the wilderness (ending the session), or use it to sneak back into Basilia to regroup at Armand's Inn.
PART 7: THE CONSULATE CRUSADE (THE NEXT DAY)
The Scene: If the party sneaks back into the Rusty Hook Inn using Pavel's magic, Armand ushers them into the back room.
* The Reveal: Armand drops the bartender act. He reveals his true identity as Envoy Shass, a high-ranking assassin for the Consulate Crusade.
* The Lore Drop: Shass acknowledges the anomalies happening around the city. He hints at Doctor Tonn's underground experiments.
* The Mission: Shass sees that the party just survived the Jewl Pirates and broke out of the Manor. He hires them. Counselor Quessel is setting sail for the capital (Auralis) tomorrow. Shass wants them to stow away on Quessel's ship and assassinate him at sea. (He notes there are Consulate members on board to help).
* The Reward (Dramatic Irony): Shass promises to reward them with potions that will "make them immensely powerful."
   * DM Note: The players should immediately realize that Shass is offering them the dangerous Doctor Tonn mutation potions!
DM POST-SESSION WRAP UP: End the session on the players accepting or debating Shass's offer. If they accept, Session 4 will begin with a stealth-heist to board Quessel's ship!

Kehrfuffle grew up in the quiet village of Ivory Hollow, a beautiful town of Wood Elves that often saw visitors seeking tranquility. His charm brought many to his shows but he was often left dissatisfied feeling that he was not getting his big break as an artist, which he felt he deserved. Despite feeling disappointed by his own lack of fame, he found love in a beautiful wood elf named Caroline who truly loved his music and believed in him. While Kehrfuffle himself could draw a crowd, Caroline was even more immensely charming and it led to the two being a highly respected couple, even being appointed to the town council in Ivory Hollow. Kehrfuffle had a good life, but he failed to recognize that at the time, driven by his own ambition. 


Until a night that changed everything.


Finishing up a gig at the Root pub, a local tavern to Ivory Hollow, Kehrfuffle was beckoned to a dimly lit table at the back of the pub. There sat a hooded man who seemed small in stature. The man’s voice seemed like a whisper and yet every word hit Kehrfuffle’s ears clearly. He offered Kehrfuffle a deal: “Undeniable musical talent in exchange for the most charming thing he had.”
Kehrfuffle laughed it off thinking the man to be a strange fan of his, but on the off chance this mysterious stranger had a prestigious job to offer him Kehrfuffle was willing to accept, believing he was merely trading a physical object this man had his eye on. When his hand met the stranger’s in agreement he felt a grip he had never felt before. The man was instantly gone and Kehrfuffle was instantly transported back to his home. On the floor in front of him lay his wife’s apron next to a beautiful accordion. The accordion pulsing as if it were alive. The painful moments that followed led to the grave realisation that the man had taken the most charming thing about him, his beloved wife Caroline. Even now the accordion occasionally plays a sad song of its own, the notes humming in the key of his missing wife. Kehrfuffle proceeded to live for years fueled by anger and a desire for vengeance. He hunted the mysterious man, occasionally hearing talk of the shrouded man who appears where ambition and desperation meet, the same verse always recited:

“A lover desperate for a bride.”
“A warrior desperate for victory.”
“A singer desperate for fame.”


His search led him back to the ancient druidic ruins of the Weeping Grove, just outside Ivory Hollow. The wild magic of the grove reacted negatively to the presence of Caroline’s accordion. The shrine that had stood for years, an attraction drawing many passersby, collapsed, crushing Kehrfuffle’s left leg entirely. After coming to, he discovered his leg had been mysteriously replaced by a thick branch, being gnawed on by an unnaturally small bear cub. The cub has never left his side since. Additionally, a large and somewhat rusty Sicle lay in the dirt next to him, while holding the Sickle it feels as though Kerfuffle can hear the heartbeat or the vegetation around him and he notices a faint glow the symbols carved into the blade’s hilt. Taking this event as a sign from the druidic force Gesma whom the shrine had been made for, Kehrfuffle re-assessed his approach. While he works to increase in strength, he also looks for allies or heroes he can manipulate into helping him, alongside his quest for clues.


It has been a while since Kehrfuffle has heard talk of the shrouded stranger. While his approach may have changed, his goal remains the same and continues to consume him. Find the stranger. Bring Caroline back.


His appearance: 
Appears to have given up caring for himself, but was once a handsome wood elf. Piercing grieving eyes. Dark hair and a beard. Slightly tattered clothes that were once nice. A locket with Caroline’s picture. Left leg is a budding thick branch. A leather bag which he often uses for carrying the little bear which he refuses to name. A branch as a peg leg that seems to be budding. And a core feature, the sandy accordion bearing the color of his wife’s hair.

The Great Arkla War
The Nevari were once viewed as the gardeners of Arkla. They kept the peace between the various races. The way they did this was through the outlawing of magic of any kind regardless of a creature’s species. There were 6 Nevari known in Arkla. Demud, Kola, Tharo, Amia, Homul and Lod. They each wielded powerful magic.


Dragons were scarce but also co-existed peacefully with everyone for a time. However tension had built up over years and Dragons felt their magical ‘wings’ were clipped by Nevari, who had a strict ban on any magic being wielded other than their own.


A dark blue dragon by the name of Clarion was born to Hesala, the Dragon queen at the time. Clarion had immense magical power from birth, his body pulsing with a magic aura unlike anything seen before. 


Concerned by this the Nevari kept a careful eye on Clarion, but this interest was misconstrued as oppressive. Clarion as he grew felt these imposing figures were holding him back and began to reach out using his magical charm to various weaker life forms. He practiced manipulating their minds, which seemed to cause an increase in lawlessness that the Nevari noticed. Demud, viewed as the wisest and most insightful Nevari despite being the smallest and weakest physically, discovered this magic to be emanating from Clarion’s nest. Attempting to confront Clarion alone, this led to a drawn out battle during which Clarion crossed a line that had never been crossed in Arkla’s history. Clarion killed Demud, a Nevari.


This led to the Nevari attempting to seal away all Dragons, which eventually they were successful in doing with the assistance of Hearn and his heroes. Each Nevari was heavily injured during this, but seeing Emperor Hearn crowned and seeing his power the Nevari agreed Arkla was in good hands. In order to prevent anything like the Dragon rebellion occurring again they decided to withdraw from Arkla, they went dormant.
