# Subject: Strider Canon - Ruth Tudul
System Log Initialized: 2026-07-07 10:57:08

RUTH TUDUL DEATH — NARRATIVE BEAT (Strider's Canon Backstory)

This event drives Edmund "Strider" Tudul's entire character arc.

SETUP:
- Edmund, Haven, and Ruth were the three Tudul children of Lord Henry Tudul.
- Edmund (eldest) = compassionate, Ruth (youngest) = playful, Haven (middle) = athletic/courageous.
- Father Henry was rarely present; the boys were raised on stories from Emperor Hearn and Commander Goron.
- Ruth loved surprising Haven, would often sneak aboard his ship to scare him.

THE INCIDENT:
- Haven returned from a "company trip" (Tudul illegal trade run) a changed man.
- Edmund was waiting on shore. Ruth had run ahead to board the ship to surprise Haven.
- A loud crash was heard. Haven disembarked, shell-shocked, unresponsive.
- Edmund approached. Haven punched him to the ground and said: "We can't change what we are."
- Lord Henry Tudul disembarked holding Ruth's limp body. He tossed her into the sea without grief.

AFTERMATH:
- Edmund fled Auralis to Chauzy, taken in by his uncle (Byron Tudul?).
- He rejected the Tudul name, adopting "Strider" to make a name that would make Ruth proud.
- Haven became more deeply entangled with Clarion's influence (the coin he picked up on that trip).
- The mystery: What happened on that ship? Was Ruth killed accidentally by Haven? Was it a sacrifice? A consequence of Haven's first deal with Clarion? The answer is deliberately withheld for dramatic reveal.

NARRATIVE FUNCTION:
- Strider's core motivation: escape the Tudul shadow, make Ruth proud, avoid facing his family.
- Haven's core wound: guilt/denial about Ruth's death, leading him deeper into Clarion's influence.
- Future confrontation between the brothers is inevitable.
